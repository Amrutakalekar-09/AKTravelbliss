Rajasthan- Jaipur

About this destination

Rajasthan is known as the 'Land of Kings'. a visit to India's largest state is an amazing chance to rediscover the grandeur of times gone by. In Jaipur, Jodhpur and Jaisalmer you can explore the region magnificent regal history whilst touring thousand-year-old forts and palaces. Amongst nine UNESCO world-heritage sites, Rajasthan is home to the world-famous Amber Palace.Visit at either sunrise or sunset for the chance to witness a breathtaking of Jaipur.

Another reason to visit Rajasthan is the chance to soak in the atmosphere at one of its many festivals.In November, you can witness the Pushkar camel fair a fascinating blend of camel trading with a jolly carnival atmosphere. Or perhaps, in January you might be able to stop by Jaipur for the International Kite Festival. Why not arrive a few days early to the spectacularly colourful Holi festival in March for Brij Holi.

Explore Rajasthani agricultural life as you stroll through villages accompanied by camels and cattle. Understand the unique pace of life in this fascinating part of India and the beautiful cultures that live there. Discover quaint mud houses and thatched roofs in Khuri, one of Rajasthan's many picturesque hamlets. Don't be surprised if you see cows being fed the first freshly cooked chapatti of the day.

Rajasthan is home to world famous wildlife and is an amazing place to witness the often-under-appreciated biodiversity India offers. At Ranthambore National Park, you'll get the chance to see some of India's unique wildlife amongst 10th century forts as you explore the park on a 4 x 4 safari. India is one of the finest places in the world to go birdwatching.

Make sure you have your cameras at the ready as you explore the cities of Rajasthan. Each city has its own unique colour profile whether that be the spectacular blues of Jodhpur or the pink city of Jaipur. Truly, the colourful nature of the landscape reflects the vibrancy of Rajasthani culture and adds to the unique experience of a visit to the northern state. Of course, it makes for the perfect photo opportunity.

Dhruv Rathi
14 Sep 2023

I went to Jaipur from Delhi with my family by flight and it took approximately 30 minutes to reach the destination. When I arrived at a colourful city I was in for a bit of shock. Of course, it was a pleasant shock, don't get confused. The part of the shock was how different it was from the image that comes to mind when one imagines Rajasthan. The view was very different from the one which we see in calendars.The majestic Amber Fort was constructed with the stunning marbles and red sandstones 200 years before Jaipur was established. These days tourists can explore the fort sitting on the back of an elephant but we have chosen to walk and see the place. Amber Fort is considered to be the famous sightseeing destination in Jaipur and you should not miss it.Second most beautiful place to see in Jaipur is non-other than Hawa Mahal. However, due to the lack of time, we checked this place just from outside as it is right on the main road.The place often come into light because of the famous dargah of “Ajmer Sharif”. And of course, you can explore Shreenathji temple on your way to Ajmer from Jaipur.

At around 1.00 pm I reached at Udaipur but bus dropped a little far away from main city. I dropped from bus and took the auto. Now It is time to search the hotel.Then I got to know that Lake Pichola is just near to my hostel. I went up there and I wanted to take that boat ride in lake Pichola. I booked the ticket at the counter and sat in boat. It was medium size motor boat. I need to wait to fill the boat for almost half an hour. Then, that boat was filled with other tourists and it started to roam around the lake. In this beautiful ride, you can see outer side of City Palace, Taj Lake Palace, Jag Mandir Palace and entire view of Lake and surroundings nearby lake. Mostly, people visit Udaipur because of the unique feel of Lake Pichola.After that boat ride, I went to Bagore Ki Haveli to enjoy a beautiful Rajasthan Cultural Dance show. Bagore ki Haveli is located on lake Pichola. It plays everyday from 6.00 PM to 7.00 PM in Bagore ki Haveli (For Indian and Foreigners both). In this show, folk performers perform traditional Rajasthani dances like Ghumar Dance, Puppet show and many cultural dances.
