About this destination

Whether you're in Downtown Dubai around the Burj Khalifa, the tallest man-made structure on earth, or down at the marina, home to four of the five tallest residential buildings in the world, you're never too far away from an amazing view.At nearly twice the height of New York's Empire State building, the tallest free-standing structure in the world towers above the clouds.

The Dubai Mall is the second biggest shopping centre on the planet by land mass, and it's just one of the many novelty centres you can visit. People travel to Dubai from near and far to indulge in retail therapy and visit the attractions built into the various shopping centres. Dubai even holds a month-long shopping festival in January with concerts and giveaways where shoppers can win cars and gold.

Dubai is a city where anything is possible - even skiing in the snow. With such a diverse and international population, there is a spectacular range of food styles available in the city. Throw in the array of food festivals that happen throughout the year and the stream of pop-up eateries and it's no wonder expats talk about newcomers putting on weight, aka the 'Dubai stone'.

The Palm Fountain is a really fun sight for anyone but, of course, the kids in particular love witnessing the magic! Designed to show the harmony between the universe and earth, the fountain puts on a colourful display where the water dances, leaps and shoots high into the sky in time with a soundtrack of music.

With almost 200 different nationalities calling the United Arab Emirates city home, and a massive 90% of the population coming from outside the country, Dubai is arguably one of the most cosmopolitan cities in the world. In contrast to cities like New York or London that have been inhabited for hundreds of years, Dubai's expats are mainly first-generation, bringing with them a host of different cultural traditions, food and experiences. The diversity on show in the city is unparalleled.

Dhruv Rathi
14 Sep 2023

Dubai, a city of dreams in the heart of the United Arab Emirates, is a place where the modern meets the traditional, and the futuristic skyline is juxtaposed against the vast desert landscape. My recent visit to Dubai was a journey of discovery, as I explored its diverse attractions and experienced its unique blend of culture and luxury. One of the first things that struck me about Dubai was its stunning skyline, dominated by the iconic Burj Khalifa, the tallest building in the world. As I stood at the base of this architectural marvel, I was awestruck by its sheer height and elegance. Another highlight of my trip was visiting the Dubai Mall, one of the largest shopping malls in the world. Here, I indulged in some retail therapy, browsing through a wide array of shops offering everything from high-end fashion to local handicrafts. No visit to Dubai is complete without experiencing its rich culture and heritage. I visited the Dubai Museum, located in the Al Fahidi Fort, which offers a fascinating glimpse into Dubai's history and evolution from a small fishing village to a global metropolis.

Isha Sharma
14 Sep 2023

One of the highlights of my trip was visiting the Dubai Marina, a stunning waterfront development lined with skyscrapers, restaurants, and cafes. I took a leisurely stroll along the marina promenade, soaking in the views of the yachts and the bustling city skyline. I also visited the historic district of Al Fahidi, where I explored the narrow lanes and traditional wind-tower houses. The Al Fahidi Fort, home to the Dubai Museum, offered a fascinating glimpse into Dubai's history and culture. No visit to Dubai is complete without experiencing its vibrant souks. I spent hours wandering through the Gold Souk, marveling at the intricate gold jewelry on display, and the Spice Souk, where the aroma of exotic spices filled the air. One of the most memorable experiences of my trip was taking a dhow cruise along the Dubai Creek. As the sun set, I enjoyed a traditional Arabic dinner onboard the dhow, while taking in the stunning views of the city skyline. Dubai's culinary scene is as diverse as its culture, with a wide range of dining options to suit every taste.
