About this destination

Maldives will be the laid-back time on the beaches without any trace of littering, but the vast bluish green Indian Ocean kissing the horizon. While mornings with soft sunlight and calmer waves are blissful, the twilight hours with thousand hues of red, orange and purple are magical. Enjoy the sea breeze and the sound of the waves in constant 25-30-degree centigrade temperature. Redefine romance under the gazillion stars.

The majestic and enormous whale sharks along with the smaller sized reef sharks lurking peacefully in the coral atolls and also on the surrounding lagoons of the resorts are the popular reasons to travel to Maldives. Although these sharks are endemic throughout the area, Baa and Ari atolls and Maaya Thila are the prospective places to encounter them up close. You can also enjoy watching them coming punctually during their daily feeding time near some resorts.

Maldives will be to make the best out of water sports like kayaking, canoeing, sea bobbing, jet-skiing, snorkeling, rollerblading etc.The dolphin and turtle safari boats should also be put on bucket list. Surfers of all levels would love the sunshine, monsoon wind and the consistent waves of the atolls like Laamu Atoll. Hands down, experiencing water sports in Maldives is one of the top things to do in the country.

One of the main reasons to visit the Maldives is its stunning natural beauty. The islands are home to some of the most beautiful beaches in the world, with powdery white sand and clear turquoise waters. Whether you're looking to relax on the beach, snorkel in the coral reefs, or simply enjoy the stunning sunsets, the Maldives has it all.

The Maldives is also known for its luxurious resorts, many of which are located on private islands. These resorts offer a level of luxury and seclusion that is hard to find elsewhere, with overwater villas, private plunge pools, and world-class dining options.the Maldives is a destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, luxury, and adventure. Whether you're looking for a romantic getaway, a family vacation, or a solo adventure, the Maldives has something for everyone.

Dhruv Rathi
14 Sep 2023

Nestled in the heart of the Maldives, the island of Male offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. My recent visit to Male was a journey of discovery, as I explored its vibrant streets and soaked in its rich heritage. One of the first things that struck me about Male was its stunning coastline. The island is surrounded by crystal-clear waters teeming with marine life, making it a paradise for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. I had the opportunity to explore the colorful coral reefs and swim alongside exotic fish, an experience I will never forget. As I wandered through the streets of Male, I was captivated by its bustling markets and lively atmosphere. The local markets are a feast for the senses, with vendors selling fresh spices, and handicrafts. I couldn't resist some of the local delicacies, including hedhikaa, a traditional Maldivian snack. One of the highlights of my trip was visiting the Maldives Islamic Centre, the largest mosque in the Maldives. The mosque's stunning architecture and intricate details were truly breathtaking, and I spent hours exploring its grounds and learning about its history.
