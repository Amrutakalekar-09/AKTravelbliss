About this destination

The city's canals, historic architecture, and world-class museums attract millions of visitors every year. One of the main attractions is the Rijksmuseum, which houses an impressive collection of Dutch art, including masterpieces by Rembrandt and Vermeer.Amsterdam, the capital city of the Netherlands, is a vibrant and historic city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and modernity.

One of the main attractions of Amsterdam is its famous canals, which crisscross the city and are a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the canals, take a boat tour, or simply sit at one of the many cafes and watch the world go by. The city is known for its vibrant culture, with a thriving arts and music scene. Visitors can explore galleries and theaters, or simply soak up the atmosphere in one of the city's many cafes and bars.

Not to be confused with coffee shops, Amsterdam's "cafes" are more like pubs rather than a place to sip a coffee. Also known as Dutch brown cafes (bruin cafes), the nicotine-stained walls and cozy wooden decor of the cafes are popular spots among locals to grab a beer or jenever, and a deep fried snack.

Making up the area known locally as De Wallen, Amsterdam's infamous Red Light District isn't for the faint of heart. The area's history of prostitution dates back to the 1300s when the city was a major trading harbor. Brothels have been legal in Amsterdam since 2000, where over 290 red-lit window beckon visitors.

Hop on a two-wheeler and ride around the most bike-friendly city in the world! With nearly 500 miles of cycle paths and bike lanes, biking in Amsterdam is by far the most popular (and efficient) way to get around. You can rent a bike from any one of the numerous bike rental companies around the city for a day rate, or you can take a guided cycle tour.Amsterdam is a city that offers a unique and unforgettable experience. Whether you're interested in history, culture, or simply want to explore a beautiful and vibrant city, Amsterdam has something for everyone.

Travel-Blog
Dhruv Rathi
14 Sep 2023

Amsterdam's iconic canals are more than just a picturesque backdrop; they are the heart and soul of the city. My recent visit to Amsterdam was a dream come true, and one of the highlights was exploring the city's enchanting canals. I started my day with a leisurely stroll along the canal banks, taking in the stunning views of the historic buildings that line the water. The architecture in Amsterdam is truly unique, with many buildings dating back centuries and featuring intricate facades and gabled roofs. Afterwards, I decided to take a canal cruise to see the city from a different perspective. As the boat glided along the tranquil waters, I was mesmerized by the beauty of Amsterdam's canals. The boat passed under bridges and past picturesque houseboats, giving me a glimpse into everyday life in this city. One of the highlights of the canal cruise was passing by the famous Anne Frank House. Seeing the house from the water gave me a new appreciation for the history of this iconic building. As the sun began to set, I made my way to one of the many canal-side cafes to enjoy a drink and watch the world go by.

Isha Sharma
14 Sep 2023

During my recent trip to Amsterdam, I had the pleasure of exploring the city's vibrant markets, each offering a unique glimpse into Dutch culture and cuisine. My first stop was the Albert Cuyp Market, one of the largest and most popular markets in Amsterdam. As I wandered through the bustling stalls, I was greeted by the sights and sounds of vendors selling everything from fresh produce to clothing and souvenirs.Next, I visited the historic Noordermarkt, a charming market located in the heart of Amsterdam's Jordaan district. Here, I found a wide array of goods, including organic produce, antiques, and handmade crafts. The market is also home to a flea market, where I spent hours browsing through unique and eclectic items. One of the highlights of my market exploration was visiting the famous floating flower market, or Bloemenmarkt. Located along the Singel canal, this market is a feast for the senses, with rows of vibrant flowers and plants on display. Exploring Amsterdam's markets was a highlight of my trip, and I would highly recommend adding them to your itinerary when visiting this beautiful city.
